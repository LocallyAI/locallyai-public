# Client Conflict Check Procedure

**Document type:** Internal procedure
**Owner:** Risk and Compliance Partner
**Applies to:** all fee earners taking on new clients or new matters

This procedure sets out how the firm identifies, escalates, and resolves potential conflicts of interest under the SRA Standards and Regulations 2019, principally Paragraphs 6.1, 6.2, and 6.5 of the Code of Conduct for Solicitors, RELs and RFLs.

## 1. When to Run a Check

A conflict check is **mandatory** before:

- accepting a new client (full client check including beneficial-owner trace);
- opening a new matter for an existing client (matter-only check);
- any material change in scope of an existing matter (e.g. additional parties, new transaction type, hostile pivot in a previously cooperative matter);
- introducing a new fee earner to an existing matter where they may have prior knowledge of an opposing party.

Checks must be completed and cleared **before** any substantive legal advice is given or any client funds are received into the firm's client account.

## 2. Information Required

The fee earner must populate the following on the conflict-check form:

- **Client identity** — full legal name (individual: forename, surname, DOB, address; entity: registered name, company number, registered office, ultimate beneficial owners ≥25%).
- **Counterparties and material third parties** — every named adverse, allied, or otherwise involved party, including their advisers where known.
- **Subject matter summary** — 100-word neutral description of the proposed work.
- **Jurisdictions** — primary and any subsidiary jurisdictions.
- **Sensitivity flags** — political exposure, sanctions exposure, public-figure clients, regulated-entity clients.

Incomplete forms are rejected by the conflict-check team without prejudice to resubmission.

## 3. Search Scope

The conflict-check team searches:

(a) the firm's matter-management system for all current and former matters within the past **6 years**;
(b) the firm-wide adverse-party index;
(c) the personal-conflict register (where partners or fee earners have declared personal interests);
(d) the firm's barrister and expert-witness instruction history (informational only — instruction does not create a conflict, but disclosure is recorded);
(e) where the proposed client is a regulated entity, the FCA register and Companies House for ownership/control changes since last check.

Search results are returned to the fee earner with a **traffic-light classification**:

- **Green** — no hits or only de minimis hits (e.g. unrelated administrative matter that has been closed for >5 years). Proceed.
- **Amber** — potential conflict. Mandatory referral to the supervising partner; analysis must be documented before proceeding.
- **Red** — actual or apparent conflict. Mandatory referral to the Risk and Compliance Partner; matter cannot be opened without express written waiver, ethical screen, or refusal to act.

## 4. Same-Side and Opposite-Side Conflicts

**Opposite-side conflict** (acting against a current client) is in almost all cases prohibited absent written informed consent from both clients, which the firm rarely seeks. The default position is decline.

**Same-side conflict** (acting for two clients with potentially divergent interests, e.g. joint venture parties) requires:

- written informed consent from both clients addressing the specific matter scope;
- a documented analysis showing that the firm can act fairly for both;
- a defined exit plan if interests later diverge mid-matter.

**Former-client conflict** under SRA Paragraph 6.5 turns on whether the firm holds confidential information from the former client material to the new matter. Where it does, an effective information barrier (ethical wall) is required, recorded on the matter, and confirmed by both partners.

## 5. Information Barriers (Ethical Walls)

Where an ethical wall is required:

- the matter is flagged in the case-management system to restrict access to named team members only;
- physical files (where any) are stored in a locked area accessible to the named team only;
- email distribution lists exclude non-team members;
- supervising partner attests in writing that the wall is in place before substantive work begins;
- annual audit confirms the wall remains effective.

Breach of an ethical wall is a serious internal matter requiring immediate escalation to the COLP (Compliance Officer for Legal Practice).

## 6. Documentation and Audit

All conflict-check records are retained for **10 years** from the date of the check, regardless of whether the matter was opened. Records are immutable in the matter-management system once finalised.

The Risk and Compliance Partner reviews a sample of conflict-check decisions monthly and reports trends to the firm's management committee quarterly.

## 7. Refusing Instructions

If a conflict cannot be resolved, the firm **must** decline to act. The fee earner sends a polite refusal letter (template C-2) without disclosing the existence or identity of the conflicting party. No reasoning beyond "we are unable to act on this matter due to internal review" is given.

*Internal procedure. SRA breach of Paragraph 6.1 / 6.2 / 6.5 may attract regulatory action including fines, restrictions on practice, or removal from the roll.*
