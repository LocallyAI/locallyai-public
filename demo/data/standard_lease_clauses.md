# Standard Commercial Lease Clauses (England and Wales)

**Document type:** Precedent library
**Practice area:** Real estate (commercial property)
**Jurisdiction:** England and Wales

This precedent collects clauses commonly used in full repairing and insuring (FRI) commercial leases. Clauses must be tailored to each transaction; counsel should not lift wholesale without review for the specific premises, term, and tenant covenant strength.

## 1. Rent

The Tenant shall pay the Initial Rent of £[•] per annum, plus VAT where applicable, by equal quarterly payments in advance on the usual quarter days (25 March, 24 June, 29 September, and 25 December), the first such payment being a proportionate sum from and including the Term Commencement Date to the next quarter day.

## 2. Rent Review

(a) The Initial Rent shall be reviewed on each Review Date (every fifth anniversary of the Term Commencement Date) to the higher of:

(i) the rent payable immediately before the Review Date; and
(ii) the open market rent of the Premises as at the Review Date determined in accordance with this clause.

(b) If the Landlord and Tenant cannot agree the open market rent within three months of the Review Date, either may refer the matter to an independent surveyor agreed between the parties or, failing agreement, appointed by the President of the RICS. The surveyor shall act as an expert.

(c) Pending determination, the Tenant shall continue to pay rent at the previous rate. Once determined, any shortfall is payable within 14 days, with interest at 4% above the Bank of England base rate from the Review Date.

## 3. Service Charge

The Tenant shall pay a fair and reasonable proportion of the Landlord's costs of providing services to the Building, calculated by reference to the rateable value of the Premises as a proportion of the rateable value of all lettable parts of the Building. The service charge year runs 1 April to 31 March. An estimated charge is payable quarterly in advance with year-end reconciliation within four months of year-end.

The Landlord shall not be entitled to recover under the service charge:

- costs attributable to a breach of the Landlord's obligations;
- costs of structural repair or renewal save to the extent expressly listed in the schedule of services;
- costs of marketing or letting voids;
- improvements that are not strictly required for the proper management of the Building.

## 4. Repair

The Tenant shall keep the Premises in good and substantial repair and condition, fair wear and tear excepted only where the lease so permits. The repair obligation extends to the interior, the non-structural parts, and the plant and machinery exclusively serving the Premises. Structural and exterior repair is the Landlord's responsibility, recoverable through the service charge except as carved out in clause 3.

A schedule of condition (photographic and written, attached at signing) limits the Tenant's repair obligation to the standard recorded therein.

## 5. Alterations

The Tenant shall not make:

- structural alterations (absolute prohibition);
- non-structural alterations (consent required, not to be unreasonably withheld);
- internal non-structural alterations of a minor nature including demountable partitioning (permitted without consent, subject to notice).

At expiry, the Tenant shall reinstate any alterations made if the Landlord so requires by notice given not less than six months before expiry.

## 6. User

The Permitted Use is [•] within Class E of the Town and Country Planning (Use Classes) Order 1987 (as amended). Change of use within Class E is permitted with the Landlord's consent, not to be unreasonably withheld. Use outside Class E requires planning permission and is subject to absolute consent.

## 7. Alienation

The Tenant shall not assign or sub-let part. Assignment of the whole and sub-letting of the whole are permitted with the Landlord's consent, not to be unreasonably withheld, subject to the Tenant providing an Authorised Guarantee Agreement (AGA) on assignment in accordance with the Landlord and Tenant (Covenants) Act 1995.

## 8. Insurance

The Landlord shall insure the Building against the Insured Risks for full reinstatement value, plus three years' loss of rent. The Tenant shall reimburse the Landlord's insurance premium as Insurance Rent. If the Premises are damaged by an Insured Risk and rendered unfit, the Rent and Service Charge shall be suspended until reinstatement, capped at the loss-of-rent insurance period.

## 9. Forfeiture

The Landlord may forfeit the lease for non-payment of rent (after 21 days regardless of formal demand) or for any other material breach (after notice under section 146 of the Law of Property Act 1925).

## 10. Break Clause (Tenant)

The Tenant may terminate the lease on the [•] anniversary of the Term Commencement Date by giving the Landlord not less than six months' prior written notice, provided that on the break date all rent and other sums then due have been paid and the Tenant gives vacant possession. Compliance with repair obligations is **not** a precondition (Landlord may pursue dilapidations separately post-break).
