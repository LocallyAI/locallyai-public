Dear [Counterparty],

## Re: Project [Code Name] — confidentiality undertaking

We act for [Client Name] (the "Client") in connection with the
Client's evaluation of a possible transaction involving
[counterparty / target] ("the Transaction").

In the course of the Client's evaluation, the Client has requested,
and you may provide, certain non-public information about
[counterparty / target] (the "Confidential Information"). This letter
records the Client's confidentiality undertaking in respect of that
information.

## 1. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means information furnished to the Client
or its Representatives (defined below) by you or on your behalf, in
any form, in connection with the Transaction, including:

- financial statements, management accounts, forecasts, budgets;
- customer and supplier information;
- regulatory correspondence;
- pending litigation and claims;
- HR information (excluding individual employee Personal Data unless
  specifically agreed);
- IT systems and security disclosures;
- any other information marked or identified as confidential.

It does not include information that becomes public other than
through breach of this undertaking, was lawfully in the Client's
possession before disclosure, is obtained without breach from a third
party, or is independently developed by the Client.

## 2. Use limitation

The Client shall use the Confidential Information solely for the
purpose of evaluating, negotiating, and (if proceeded) closing the
Transaction. The Client shall not use it for any other purpose,
including any business activity unrelated to the Transaction or any
trading in the securities of [counterparty / target].

## 3. Representatives

The Client may disclose the Confidential Information to its
"Representatives" — namely:

(a) the Client's directors, officers, employees, and consultants who
    have a genuine need to know for the Transaction;
(b) the Client's external counsel (including this firm), accountants,
    bankers, and other professional advisers; and
(c) prospective financing sources approved in advance by you in
    writing.

The Client is responsible for any breach by its Representatives. Each
Representative receiving Confidential Information shall be advised of
its confidential nature and shall be bound by terms substantially
equivalent to this letter, whether by professional duty (in the case
of counsel and accountants) or by signed engagement.

## 4. Personal Data

To the extent the Confidential Information contains Personal Data
within the meaning of the Saudi PDPL (Royal Decree M/19, 2023):

- you warrant that you have lawful basis to disclose it for the
  Transaction purpose;
- the Client and its Representatives shall process it only for that
  purpose and in accordance with PDPL Arts. 18–23;
- the Client and its Representatives shall not transfer the Personal
  Data outside KSA without your prior written consent and compliance
  with PDPL Art. 29.

## 5. Term

This undertaking continues for **two (2) years** from the date of
this letter, except that obligations in respect of any Personal Data
or trade secrets continue for the period required by applicable law.

## 6. Return / destruction

On your written request (or on termination of negotiations without
the Transaction proceeding), the Client and its Representatives shall
return or destroy all Confidential Information, retaining only:

- copies required by professional rules of the Saudi Bar Association
  governing client file retention;
- archival copies in IT system backups not generally accessible.

Such retained copies remain subject to this undertaking.

## 7. Compulsion to disclose

If the Client or any Representative is required by law, by regulator
demand, or by court order to disclose any Confidential Information,
the Client shall:

(a) where legally permitted, give you prompt written notice so you
    may seek a protective order;
(b) co-operate (at your cost) in any reasonable steps you take to
    protect the Confidential Information; and
(c) disclose only the portion legally required.

## 8. No representation

The Confidential Information is provided "as is"; no warranty as to
accuracy or completeness is made. The Client's reliance on the
Confidential Information for closing decisions is at the Client's own
risk.

## 9. No agreement to transact

This letter does not commit the Client to enter into the Transaction
or to continue discussions. Either Party may terminate the
discussions at any time without liability.

## 10. Governing law

This letter is governed by the laws of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia
and the Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the
competent courts of Riyadh.

(Where the counterparty is incorporated outside KSA and prefers DIFC
or ADGM jurisdiction, this clause may be amended by mutual written
agreement; the Client's preference remains the courts of Riyadh.)
