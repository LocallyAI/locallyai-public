# Conflict-check procedure (KSA practice)

> **Synthetic demo document.** Internal procedure for conflict checks
> at a Saudi-based law firm. Demo seed for the Saudi LocallyAI
> deployment.

---

## 1. Why we run conflict checks

Saudi Bar Association rules and the firm's professional indemnity
insurance both require us to identify and address conflicts of
interest BEFORE accepting a new matter. A conflict that's missed at
intake becomes a conflict we have to handle mid-matter, which is
disruptive for the client and damaging for the firm.

Conflict types we screen for:

- **Direct adverse** — proposed client is on the opposite side of an
  existing or recently-closed matter.
- **Issue conflict** — the proposed matter requires us to argue a
  position that's adverse to a position we've taken or are taking
  for another client.
- **Confidential information conflict** — we hold confidential
  information about a third party that would be relevant to the
  proposed matter.
- **Personal conflict** — a fee earner has a personal interest
  (financial, family, prior employment) in the proposed matter.
- **Aggregate / commercial conflict** — the proposed client competes
  with a major existing client where representation would damage
  that relationship.

## 2. Procedure

### Step 1 — Intake form

The originating partner (or their nominated paralegal) completes the
firm's intake form, collecting:

- proposed client name(s) — including all parent / subsidiary
  entities and ultimate beneficial owners as far as known;
- proposed matter description (one-paragraph summary);
- counterparty / adverse party names (same level of corporate
  detail);
- the matter type (litigation, transactional, advisory, regulatory);
- the proposed lead fee earner and supporting team;
- expected fee structure;
- any known relationships between any individual on the proposed
  team and any party.

### Step 2 — Database search

The conflicts coordinator runs a name search across:

- the firm's matter management system (current and closed matters
  for the past **seven years**);
- the firm's relationship database (client referrals, panel
  appointments);
- where appropriate, public sources to validate corporate-group
  scope (e.g. SAMA register for financial entities, CMA register
  for listed companies, ZATCA register).

### Step 3 — Triage

Search hits are categorised:

- **Green — no conflict.** The proposed matter has no parties or
  issues overlapping with the firm's existing or recent work.
  Proceed to step 5.
- **Amber — review required.** Hits exist but may be addressable
  with information barriers or client consent. Proceed to step 4.
- **Red — likely conflict.** Hits indicate a conflict that cannot be
  easily addressed. Proceed to step 4 with a recommendation to
  decline.

### Step 4 — Senior review

The originating partner discusses amber/red conflicts with the head
of the relevant practice group and (for material commercial conflicts)
the Managing Partner. Outcomes:

- **Cleared with no measures** — green-light to proceed.
- **Cleared with information barrier** — internal Chinese-wall
  arrangement documented; named individuals identified; physical
  and electronic access controls in place.
- **Cleared with client consent** — written consent from each
  affected client obtained before formal engagement.
- **Declined** — the firm does not accept the proposed matter; the
  reason is recorded confidentially.

### Step 5 — Engagement

Once cleared, the originating partner proceeds with engagement. The
intake record is finalised and stored in the firm's matter management
system.

## 3. Information barriers

When a matter proceeds with an information barrier:

- A list of "screened individuals" who must NOT have access to the
  matter is published to IT, who applies access-control rules to
  the matter file in the document management system.
- Physical workspace separation is reviewed (different floor / wing
  preferred for sensitive cases).
- The lead lawyer briefs the matter team on the barrier and the
  consequences of a breach.
- A breach (intentional or accidental) is reported to the Managing
  Partner immediately and triggers a review of whether the matter
  can continue.

## 4. PDPL considerations

The intake form collects Personal Data about the proposed client's
representatives, the counterparty's representatives, and (where
relevant) third parties. Lawful basis: contract performance with the
proposed client (PDPL Art. 5(1)(b)) and the firm's legitimate
interest in compliance (Art. 5(1)(f)).

The intake records are retained for the period required for client-
file retention under Saudi Bar rules, and access is restricted to
the conflicts coordinator, the relevant practice group head, and
(for the relevant matter) the matter team.

## 5. Cross-firm conflicts (international engagements)

Where the proposed matter involves a firm in another jurisdiction
(co-counsel arrangements, international transactions), the conflict
check covers:

- our firm's database, AND
- to the extent the foreign firm has agreed to share, the foreign
  firm's database (under reciprocal NDA).

A conflict identified at the foreign firm is treated as if at our
firm.

## 6. Escalation

Disagreements between the originating partner and the conflicts
coordinator are escalated to the practice group head; further
disagreements to the Managing Partner. The Managing Partner's
decision is final.

## 7. Audit and review

The Conflicts Office conducts an annual review of conflict
decisions, particularly amber-cleared and information-barrier
matters, to verify the controls remained effective. Findings are
reported to the Managing Partner and feed into staff training.

## 8. Confidentiality of the conflict check itself

The fact that a conflict check has been run, and its outcome, are
themselves confidential. The conflicts coordinator does NOT discuss
declined matters with anyone outside the senior review group.
Information about declined matters is not retained beyond the
minimum necessary.
