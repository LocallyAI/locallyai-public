**Purpose:** discussion and evaluation of a potential commercial

## 1. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed
by or on behalf of the Discloser to the Recipient, in any form
(written, oral, visual, electronic), including but not limited to:

- business and financial information (forecasts, customer lists,
  pricing, margins, revenue);
- strategic plans, transaction terms, valuation models;
- technical information (software, processes, methodologies,
  trade secrets);
- personal data of the Discloser's personnel or counterparties
  insofar as required for the Purpose; and
- the existence and content of discussions between the Parties.

Confidential Information does NOT include information that:

(a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this NDA;
(b) was lawfully in the Recipient's possession before disclosure;
(c) is lawfully obtained from a third party with no confidentiality
    obligation; or
(d) is independently developed by the Recipient without reference to
    the Confidential Information.

## 2. Permitted use

The Recipient shall:

- use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
- not disclose to any third party without prior written consent;
- protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree
  of care as it uses for its own most sensitive information, and in
  any event no less than reasonable care;
- limit access to its directors, officers, employees, and
  professional advisers (collectively, "Representatives") who have a
  genuine need to know for the Purpose, and who are bound by
  equivalent confidentiality obligations.

The Recipient is responsible for any breach by its Representatives.

## 3. Personal data and PDPL compliance

To the extent the Confidential Information includes Personal Data
within the meaning of the **Saudi Personal Data Protection Law
(Royal Decree M/19, 2023)** ("PDPL"):

- the Discloser warrants that it has lawful basis under PDPL Art. 5
  to disclose the Personal Data to the Recipient for the Purpose;
- the Recipient shall process the Personal Data solely for the
  Purpose, in accordance with PDPL Art. 18–23 obligations applicable
  to processors;
- the Recipient shall not transfer the Personal Data outside the
  Kingdom of Saudi Arabia without the Discloser's prior written
  consent and only where PDPL Art. 29 cross-border conditions
  (including SDAIA approval where required) are satisfied;
- the Parties shall co-operate in good faith on any data subject
  request received by either Party in respect of the Personal Data.

## 4. Duration and survival

The Recipient's obligations of confidentiality and non-use survive
termination of this NDA for a period of **five (5) years** from the
date of last disclosure, except that obligations in respect of trade
secrets continue indefinitely for as long as the information remains a
trade secret.

## 5. Return or destruction

On the Discloser's written request, the Recipient shall promptly:

(a) return all Confidential Information in its possession or control,
    or
(b) destroy it (including all copies, summaries, derivations, and
    notes incorporating the Confidential Information), and certify
    such destruction in writing.

The Recipient may retain copies required by applicable law or its
auditors' professional standards, subject to ongoing
confidentiality.

## 6. No licence; no representation

Nothing in this NDA grants the Recipient any licence or right under
the Discloser's intellectual property. The Confidential Information is
provided "as is"; no representation as to accuracy or completeness is
made.

## 7. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that monetary damages may be inadequate for
breach. Each Party may seek injunctive relief in addition to any
other remedy available at law or in equity, including specific
performance. This is without prejudice to any contractual remedies in
the underlying commercial agreement (if any).

## 8. Governing law and jurisdiction

This NDA is governed by the laws of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

The Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the competent
courts of Riyadh, KSA.

(For DIFC / ADGM / international counterparties, the Parties may
alternatively select DIFC Courts or ADGM Courts as applicable; the
Discloser's preferred forum is the courts of Riyadh.)

## 9. Notices

Notices must be in writing, in English (or English with Arabic
translation; the Arabic version controls in case of conflict before
KSA courts), and delivered to the addresses set out above.

## 10. Entire agreement; amendments

This NDA is the entire agreement between the Parties on the subject
matter and supersedes prior negotiations. Amendments must be in
writing and signed by both Parties.
